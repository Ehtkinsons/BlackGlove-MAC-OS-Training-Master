Organization-Owned Model

In an organization-owned model, you purchase devices from Apple or a participating Apple Authorized Reseller or carrier. You then provide a device to each user (personally enabled) or share devices among users (nonpersonalized).

By using a combination of these strategies, key technologies from Apple, and an MDM solution, you can fully automate device setup and configuration.

Personally Enabled

In a personally enabled strategy, your organization provides a device to each user. For devices purchased directly from Apple or participating Apple Authorized Resellers or carriers, you can use Apple Business Manager or Apple School Manager to automatically enroll new devices in your MDM solution.

After you configure the devices, users can personalize their devices with their own apps and data in addition to the organization’s accounts or apps.

Nonpersonalized

When users share a device for a single purpose (for example, a kiosk device in a retail store or a hotel), IT administrators typically configure and manage them centrally. With a nonpersonalized device deployment, IT administrators restrict the installed apps and personal data on the device.

Shared iPad

In iPadOS 13.4 or later, Shared iPad lets any user start a temporary session without entering a user name or password. Users can tap Guest at the login screen and use the device. Shared iPad deletes guest user data and browser history when the guest user logs out.

Guest users in a temporary session need to consider the temporary nature of their data if they’re signed in to any websites or apps. Because guest users don’t use a Managed Apple ID, temporary sessions may not support apps that use or require iCloud or cloud-based storage. Guest users can’t modify account settings or sign in to Apple services during a temporary session on Shared iPad.

Note

Because you can assign Apple Books purchases only to users with an Apple ID or Managed Apple ID, you can’t assign books in a temporary session on Shared iPad.

User Enrollment Model

When users bring their personal devices — commonly referred to as a “bring your own device” (BYOD) deployment — you can still provide access to organization services such as Wi-Fi, mail, and calendars with MDM. Users set up and configure their devices after they enroll them in your organization’s MDM solution. Enrolling grants users access to organization resources such as configuration profiles, apps, and security settings. This enrollment type uses Managed Apple IDs, which organizations own and manage. Managed Apple IDs provide the following:

  • User access to Apple services

  • Sign-in credentials for Apple School Manager or Apple Business Manager roles

You can create Managed Apple IDs manually or automatically if you use federated authentication.

User Enrollment integrates with Managed Apple ID to establish a user identity on the device. The Managed Apple ID is part of the User Enrollment profile, and the user must successfully sign in to complete enrollment. Users can use both their Managed Apple ID and their personal Apple ID on the device without conflict.

Device Enrollment

When users enroll their personal devices in the MDM solution, IT administrators can manage many different aspects of device use, including the ability to erase the device. Device Enrollment also offers IT administrators a larger set of payloads and restrictions they can apply to the device. When a user removes an enrollment profile, they also remove all configuration profiles, settings, and managed apps related to the enrollment profile.

For more details about ownership models, refer to Designing a Security Strategy.
